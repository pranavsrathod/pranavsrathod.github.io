PROJECT 2 - Pow! A Virtual Comic Shop and Arcade

A Virtual Reality Project made using Unity and Virtual Reality Toolkit(VRTK), to give an immersive experice to interact with objects sold and displayed in an imaginary, vitual comic shop and arcade in the future CS building at UIC

Pranav Rathod and Ish Soni

Project 2
October 17, 2022

To use this app you must have Unity 2021.3.6f1 (go here https://unity3d.com/get-unity/download/archive and find the version and download via unity hub) and VRTK version 4 (it is already included in the files so no need to worry about installing this) and download the app files from the git repository

Link To The Project : https://github.com/Dragon0715/Ish-Soni-Pranav-Rathod-Project-2

After extracting the zip file, open the file in unity and go into assets/CoffeeShopStarterPack/Scenes and open up the CoffeeShop Scene. From here you can see all the files that we used and you can begin using the app in the editor. The scene is of a comic/game kiosk in the new CS building that’s selling various game and comic related items.

To be able to run the app, make sure you have CameraRigs.UnityXRPluginFramework disabled and you have CameraRigs.SpatialSimulator enabled. Once the scene builds, use WASD to move around and the mouse to look around. You can also use 2 and 3 to take control of the left and right controllers respectively and move them around with WASD and the mouse. You can grab and interact with various objects in the scene as well. To run the app in VR, first you need to have CameraRigs.UnityXRPluginFramework enabled and CameraRigs.SpatialSimulator disabled.

Then connect your oculus headset and under Unity Build Settings make sure you are building for the Android Platform and the particular Quest you connected should show up under the Run Device list of compatible connected devices. If the Quest headset does not show up in the list you may need to unplug and re-plug the USB cable and again tell the quest to accept the connection to the laptop. You may also need to make sure USB debugging is enabled on the quest.

You can check this by clicking on the Quick Settings menu on the left side of the thin menu, then clicking on the settings menu on the upper right, then scrolling down in the menu on the left to Developer and turning on the USB Connection Dialog. After clicking build and run, wait for the app to build and it should start automatically on the quest. From there you can experience the scene in VR either via walking around the scene or teleporting around, being able to interact with the same objects as you could when running the scene in the editor with the oculus touch controllers.

How viewing and interacting with your world is different in the simulator and the headset?

Ish Soni :

Viewing and interacting with the kiosk in VR is vastly different than when you are doing the same thing via the Unity simulator. In the simulator it is similar to playing a normal pc game where you can use WASD to move around the world. It then becomes very different as you have to use the 2 and 3 keys to switch between the player and the oculus controllers. After switching to them you can use the same controls to move the controllers around the scene and rotate them with the mouse. By clicking the left mouse button you can grab objects that are interesting with your controller. It is kind of jarring to play in the simulator as it becomes difficult at times to move the controllers into the right spot to interact with certain items in the scene.

The experience in VR is a lot smoother and easier to interact with the scene. The first main difference is that you can teleport around the scene as well as walk around the space by walking in the real world. It is also much easier to interact with the various items in the scene because you can just move your hand to that location and not have to deal with trying to have the controller point in the right direction with your mouse and then move it with the WASD keys. Playing in VR also gives you a sense of scale with the scene. You can easily see the different sizes of the items in comparison to your own height which is something that cannot be replicated via the simulator. It also gives you a sense of spatial awareness as well as you can see everything around you as if you were actually there. The 3D sounds help this too since you can physically get closer to the objects playing these sounds and hear them get louder as you do. This is something you can experience in the simulator as well but I personally think it has a bigger impact on your spatial awareness in the VR headset rather than in the editor. Playing the scene in VR lets you experience the physics of the objects much better than in the simulator. While the simulator lets you pick up objects, move them around, and bump them into other objects it doesn’t give you a full sense of the physics. Whereas when you are in VR you can do all of the things in the simulator as well as toss things around among other things that will display the physics much more than you can fully simulate with the controllers in the simulator.

Because of all of these reasons I believe that being able to view our project in VR makes it a much better experience overall than just being able to view it in the simulator. While the simulator does a good job of mimicking the VR headset and the controllers it is best for testing out the scene. The full experience should be saved for the VR headset as it will give you the best feel of the scene that you have created.

Pranav Rathod :

Through the process of working on this project we were able to gain understanding about how space constraints can hinder the experience of interacting with a virtual world.

I was reminded of when I started learning to drive as soon as I turned 18 years old. I had joined a driving school that taught me the basic concepts of driving on a simulator, which was just like the ones we used to have in mall arcades.

When learning on a simulator, I learnt how to change gears in a manual (stick shift) car and understood how different speeds are affected by gears and the importance of having control over your vehicle when speeding up and slowing down. This played a crucial part in my learning before I started practicing driving in the real world which would have been dangerous when learning how to drive in a crowded city that also has bad traffic. But the basics that were taught on the simulator were very limited.

When I started driving on roads I had a hard time having spatial awareness of what is going around me because until then I was confined to the knowledge that I learnt on 3 screens that was not immersive and intuitive at all. I was thrown off by how, in real life you need to be hyperware of your surroundings to be the best at driving. The cars that you can drive past can be super close and only experience can enhance the judgment for you while driving in real time.

This was a similar difference that we noticed when working on both the simulator and the headset. Working on the simulator helped us save time in placing the models in the given space, but using the headset helped us understand the scale of the objects with respect to each other, we understood that purpose of the headset was to place our own self into the environment and interact with the objects, like grab them, toss them hold them closer to listen what sound they make with respect to their distance from you, this kind of immersive experience was not achievable when using a simulator as it was not immersive, we were confined to the dimension of our laptop screen and the interactions that we made did not feel organic.

Using the headset we were able to move around the environment but also had its cons, such as we were only able to test the application after making major changes, since building and running the project on the Oculus Quest was time consuming, so using the simulator to see test the project after making quick changes came in handy in saving time.

I feel there is a huge difference when it comes to using simulators and placing ourselves in real world scenarios. Simulators may help us brace ourselves and give us a conceptual understanding on how things are, but it is actually being in place that makes it realistic, immersive and interesting.
